HOW PHIC WORKS

Every PHIC parameter flows through four distinct layers in this order:

1. Kelly Sizing — autonomy_level × max_position_pct × conviction × kelly 2. Metabolic Filter — hurdle = baseCost × e^(strain) × vpinMult × riskMult 3. Guardian Worker — state machine (NOMINAL/CAUTIOUS/REDUCE_ONLY/HALTED) 4. Execution Worker — stop-loss, floor, profit banking, exposure caps

A parameter in layer 1 does not bypass layers 2–4. The layers are cumulative — every trade passes through all four. This means you can set autonomy_level = 1.0 and still have zero executions if the Guardian is in HALTED or the Metabolic hurdle is too high.

GOLDEN RULE Change parameters in pairs. Most PHIC controls have a counterpart that must move with them. The cross-dependency warnings in each section tell you which ones.

CORE EXECUTION GATE

autonomy_level 0.0 – 1.0 default: 0.50 Kelly

The master throttle. Scales the Kelly fraction applied to every order. A value of 0.5 means the system uses 50% of the Kelly-optimal position size. This is the first multiplier in the sizing formula:

qty = (availUsdt × autonomy_level × (max_position_pct/100) × conviction × kelly) / price
If you raise it…If you lower it…
Larger positions. More P&L variance. Higher capital utilisation. Smaller positions. Less variance. Lower utilisation but also lower potential losses.
⚠ ALWAYS CHECK WITH max_position_pct Raising autonomy_level without capping max_position_pct can result in very large single orders. The product autonomy_level × max_position_pct/100 is the actual fraction of available USDT used per trade. At autonomy=1.0, max_position=25%, each trade uses up to 25% of free capital × Kelly fraction. At autonomy=1.0, max_position=100%, a single trade could consume all free capital.
max_position_pct 0 – 100 default: 1.0% (bridge) / 25% (browser) Kelly

Hard cap on the fraction of free USDT that any single trade can consume. Works as a ceiling, not a target — Kelly and conviction usually keep actual sizes below this.

If you raise it…If you lower it…
Larger possible orders. Faster compounding when winning. Smaller orders. Protects against single-trade blowouts but slows compounding.
⚠ AFFECTS FLOOR CALCULATION The floor that triggers emergency freeze is: floor = HWM × (1 − max_total_exposure_pct/100). max_position_pct does not appear in the floor formula — but oversized positions accumulated over many trades raise total BTC exposure, which the floor enforces globally via max_total_exposure_pct. Raise max_position_pct only alongside reviewing max_total_exposure_pct.
max_total_exposure_pct 0 – 100 default: 20% (bridge) / 60% (browser) Exec

Controls two things simultaneously — this is the most dangerous parameter to change carelessly:

1. Hard exposure cap: total BTC holdings cannot exceed this fraction of portfolio value. New buys are trimmed to fit.

2. Floor level: the emergency-freeze floor is HWM × (1 − max_total_exposure_pct/100).

At max_total_exposure_pct = 20%: floor = HWM × 0.80 (lose 20% of HWM → freeze) At max_total_exposure_pct = 60%: floor = HWM × 0.40 (lose 60% of HWM → freeze) At max_total_exposure_pct = 100%: floor = 0 (no floor at all — unlimited drawdown)
🚨 MOST DANGEROUS PARAMETER Raising this does two harm-compounding things at once: it allows bigger BTC positions AND it lowers the floor. A value of 60% means you can hold 60% of portfolio in BTC AND must lose 60% of your HWM before the system freezes. These compound: a 60% position going against you 100% would lose 60% of portfolio before the freeze fires. Never raise above 40% unless you have a specific tested reason.
  • If you raise max_total_exposure_pct, also raise stop_loss_pct to protect from large losses before the floor fires.
  • If you raise max_total_exposure_pct, consider lowering max_position_pct to prevent a single trade consuming the new headroom.
max_pattern_exposure_pct 0.0 – 1.0 (fraction of portfolio) default: 0.30 Exec

Caps BTC exposure attributable to any single pattern. Independent of max_total_exposure_pct. Prevents one strategy from dominating the book. If a pattern exceeds this, new buys from it are blocked until it shrinks via sells.

⚠ WORKS WITH REGIME CAPS Regime caps (per LowVol/HighVol/Crisis) are a size cap, not an exposure cap. max_pattern_exposure_pct is a second, orthogonal layer. Both apply simultaneously.
execution_disabled true / false default: false Exec

Silently drops all execution intents before they reach the exchange. Useful for running in live-data observation mode without any risk. Does not affect signal generation, VPIN tracking, or aliveness updates — the system continues learning while not trading.

Use execution_disabled = true when calibrating thresholds or testing new patterns. Different from emergency_freeze: freeze is reactive (triggered by drawdown), disabled is intentional (set manually before starting).

RISK & DRAWDOWN

stop_loss_pct 0.0 – 10.0 default: 2.5% Exec

Sells 50% of the BTC position when live price drops this percentage below the position's average cost. Two-minute cooldown prevents repeated triggers during a slide. Set to 0 to disable entirely.

trigger_price = avg_cost × (1 − stop_loss_pct / 100) action: sell 50% of BTC position at market
If you raise it (e.g. 2.5% → 4%)If you lower it (e.g. 2.5% → 1%)
More room for mean-reversion. More P&L at risk before stop fires. Floor may fire first on sharp drops. Tighter loss control. More stop-outs on normal volatility. SPREAD_FADE and REVERSION_A suffer most — they need price room to recover.
🚨 STOPS VS FLOOR INTERACTION If stop_loss_pct is too LOW relative to normal BTC volatility, the stop fires constantly on noise and you accumulate partial sells that erode capital without a clean exit. If it is too HIGH (or 0), the floor can fire before the stop does — the floor freezes ALL execution, not just this position.

Rule of thumb: stop_loss_pct should be set to roughly 2–3× the expected single-candle move in your typical regime (e.g., if 1-min candles move $200 on a $60k BTC, that's 0.33% — set stop to ~1.0–1.5%).
  • If you raise stop_loss_pct, also review bank_profit_threshold_pct — you want profit to be banked before the stop fires.
  • If you set stop_loss_pct = 0, lower max_total_exposure_pct so the floor provides protection.
max_drawdown_pct 0 – 100 default: 2.0% Exec

Session drawdown limit. When total realized + unrealized PnL falls below −max_drawdown_pct% of the session starting value, the system triggers emergency freeze (via drawdown_hysteresis_n consecutive breaches). Works independently from the floor.

⚠ WORKS WITH HYSTERESIS This parameter alone does not freeze. It must be breached drawdown_hysteresis_n times consecutively before the freeze fires. Setting drawdown_hysteresis_n = 1 makes this immediate; setting it to 5 gives the system time to recover naturally.
drawdown_hysteresis_n 1 – 10 default: 3 Exec

Number of consecutive drawdown-threshold breaches before emergency freeze activates. Each "breach" is one check in the periodic portfolio monitoring loop (every ~1s). Hysteresis prevents a single bad tick from freezing the system.

  • Low value (1–2) combined with low max_drawdown_pct = very sensitive freeze trigger.
  • High value (8–10) combined with high max_drawdown_pct = almost never freezes — relying on stop-loss instead.

PROFIT BANKING

Profit banking is a two-tier velocity-aware system. Gains are measured against the High-Water Mark (HWM). The HWM only advances — it never retreats. This means banked profits are locked.

Floor = HWM × (1 − max_total_exposure_pct/100) ← emergency freeze trigger Tier-1 triggers when: total_value > HWM × (1 + bank_profit_threshold_pct) Tier-1 banks: (excess above HWM) × bank_tier1_frac — immediately Tier-2 triggers when: velocity reverses OR dwell time exceeds bank_profit_dwell_min Tier-2 banks: remaining excess above (advanced) HWM
bank_profit_threshold_pct 0.0 – 10.0% (stored as fraction, e.g. 0.002 = 0.2%) default: 0.2% Exec

The gain above HWM required to trigger Tier-1 banking. Too low: banks tiny gains constantly, churning the HWM upward in small increments. Too high: misses banking during brief profit peaks.

If you raise it (e.g. 0.2% → 1.9%)If you lower it (e.g. 0.2% → 0.05%)
Larger gain required before any banking. Good for trending markets — lets profits run. Risk: peak passes without banking on short pumps. Banks more frequently on small gains. Good for choppy/ranging markets. Risk: HWM advances slowly in tiny steps, floor barely moves.
🚨 STOP-LOSS vs BANKING ORDER If stop_loss_pct < bank_profit_threshold_pct, the stop fires before any profit is ever banked. Example: stop_loss=1%, bank_threshold=2%. A trade that goes +1.8% then reverses will be stopped at -1%, never banking anything.
Always keep stop_loss_pct < bank_profit_threshold_pct × 5 as a rough guide.
bank_tier1_frac 0.1 – 1.0 default: 0.60 (60%) Exec

Fraction of the excess above HWM that is immediately locked as Tier-1 banking. The remaining fraction stays "at risk" for Tier-2 banking later.

  • At 1.0: all excess banked immediately — no Tier-2. Acts as a simple HWM ratchet. Good for volatile markets.
  • At 0.1: only 10% banked immediately. Allows most gains to compound further. Higher regret on reversals.
  • Typical range: 0.50–0.70. Balance between locking gains and letting winners compound.
bank_profit_dwell_min 1 – 60 minutes default: 10 minutes Exec

Tier-2 fallback: if the position stays above HWM for this many minutes without velocity reversing, bank the remaining excess. Prevents gains from sitting "at risk" indefinitely during slow uptrends where velocity never clearly reverses.

⚠ SHORT DWELL + HIGH THRESHOLD = NEVER BANKING If bank_profit_dwell_min = 1 and bank_profit_threshold_pct = 1.9%, the system must hit 1.9% gain AND sustain it for 1 minute. In a choppy market that's never achieved — Tier-2 never fires. Use longer dwells in choppy markets and shorter ones in trending markets where large gains happen quickly.

VPIN & REGIME

VPIN (Volume-weighted Probability of Informed trading) measures toxic order flow. A VPIN near 1.0 means all flow is one-directional (informed traders taking one side) — a sign of upcoming sharp moves. VPIN is computed every 8ms from the tick ring buffer.

vpin_crisis_threshold 0.0 – 1.0 default: 0.70 Nociceptor

Above this VPIN, the Nociceptor fires CANCEL_ORDERS and the Metabolic filter applies maximum penalty to mean-reversion signals. The regime detector transitions to Crisis. Also affects the dynamic hurdle: mean-reversion hurdle = baseExecCost × e^(1.5) × (1 + vpinOver × 8).

If you raise it (e.g. 0.70 → 0.85)If you lower it (e.g. 0.70 → 0.55)
Less sensitive to toxic flow. More executions during volatile periods. Risk: mean-reversion signals execute during genuine flush events. More sensitive. Fewer executions overall. Can be over-defensive — momentum and arb signals also get hit via regime change.
🚨 NEVER RAISE ABOVE max_crisis_threshold The calibration wizard sets this from live VPIN percentile data. If a sustained toxic period has all ticks at 0.90+ VPIN, calibration might suggest raising this to 0.95+, making the system essentially immune to CANCEL_ORDERS during a real crisis. max_crisis_threshold (default 0.90) is the hard ceiling that prevents this.
⚠ SYMMETRIC RELATIONSHIP WITH vpin_highvol_threshold The band between vpin_highvol_threshold and vpin_crisis_threshold defines the HighVol regime window. If you raise vpin_crisis_threshold, also review whether the HighVol window is still a realistic range for the instrument. A window narrower than 0.15 VPIN units is unreliable.
vpin_highvol_threshold 0.0 – 1.0 default: 0.40 Nociceptor

Regime transitions to HighVol above this VPIN. In HighVol, the regime strain exponent applies (default 0.5 → e^0.5 ≈ 1.65× hurdle for mean-reversion). Momentum and arb are unaffected by the regime strain change.

⚠ KEEP BAND ≥ 0.15 UNITS FROM vpin_crisis_threshold Example: highvol=0.55 + crisis=0.70 → 0.15 unit band. Narrower than this and VPIN spikes jump directly from LowVol to Crisis without the HighVol intermediate step. This means HighVol regime entries never appear in the echo table — patterns don't build HighVol aliveness history, leaving blind spots.
regime_caps (LowVol / HighVol / Crisis) 0 – ∞ (BTC units, leave blank for unlimited) default: LowVol=0.4, HighVol=1.0, Crisis=0.1 Exec

Maximum BTC that can be bought in a single execution per regime. Applies per trade, not cumulatively. Crisis should always be the most restrictive. Setting Crisis cap to 0 prevents all new buys in Crisis regime.

🚨 CRISIS CAP = 0 IS OFTEN CORRECT In a Crisis regime (toxic flow, sharp moves), execution of any new buy is high-risk. The Nociceptor already fires CANCEL_ORDERS above the crisis VPIN threshold, but regime caps provide a second enforcement. Consider: why are you buying during a Crisis regime? Mean-reversion in genuine Crisis flow kills accounts.
regime_strain_exp (LowVol / HighVol / Crisis) LowVol: 0.0 / HighVol: 0.0–2.0 / Crisis: 0.5–3.0 default: LowVol=0.0, HighVol=0.5, Crisis=1.5 Nociceptor

The exponent applied to the hurdle formula via e^(strain). Higher strain means the signal must produce larger edge to clear the hurdle. ARBI_CROSS_EXCHANGE is completely exempt (timing edge, not directional bet). In Crisis with strain=1.5: e^1.5 ≈ 4.5× hurdle.

Regimestrain=0.0strain=0.5strain=1.0strain=1.5strain=2.0
Hurdle mult1.0×1.65×2.72×4.48×7.39×
⚠ STRAIN AND VPIN THRESHOLD INTERACT If Crisis strain is 1.5 AND the Crisis VPIN threshold is low (e.g. 0.55), then entering Crisis regime is easy but also very punishing. The strategy ensemble will go quiet frequently. If Crisis strain is 0.5 AND the threshold is high (0.90), the system trades through most volatility spikes — suitable for momentum strategies but dangerous for mean-reversion.

CALIBRATION SAFETY

max_crisis_threshold 0.50 – 1.0 default: 0.90 Nociceptor

Hard ceiling applied to the VPIN crisis threshold after each auto-calibration run. The calibration wizard computes the p95 VPIN from recent history and proposes that as the new crisis threshold. If the market has been in sustained Crisis (all ticks at 0.92 VPIN), the calibration would suggest 0.92 — making the threshold effectively useless. This ceiling clamps the result.

Leave at 0.90 unless you have measured that your instrument regularly reaches 0.90+ VPIN in normal high-volume periods (not crisis events). Raising it means fewer CANCEL_ORDERS alerts and less protection.
strain_nack_threshold 0.0 – 1.0 default: 0.60 (60%) Guardian

If a pattern's strain_count / (strain_count + execution_count) ratio exceeds this, the Guardian NACKs all intents from that pattern for strain_cooldown_min minutes. Strain count is the number of Metabolic DROP events for the pattern (signals that lost). This prevents a pattern that is currently in a losing streak from continuing to generate trades.

If you raise it (e.g. 0.60 → 0.80)If you lower it (e.g. 0.60 → 0.40)
More tolerant of losing streaks. Pattern must be 80%+ losers before cooldown fires. Risk: more losses accumulate before suppression. Aggressively suppresses underperforming patterns. A 40% loss rate triggers cooldown. Risk: suppresses patterns that are in temporary drawdown but still have long-term edge.
⚠ REQUIRES MINIMUM SAMPLES The Guardian ignores patterns with fewer than 3 total events. A brand-new pattern or one that rarely signals will never hit the threshold. Strain cooldown only applies to patterns with established signal history.
strain_cooldown_min 1 – 30 minutes default: 5 minutes Guardian

How long a strained pattern is suppressed after exceeding the strain NACK threshold. The cooldown is renewed (extended) each time an echo snapshot arrives and the ratio is still above threshold — so a persistently bad pattern stays suppressed indefinitely.

⚠ ADJUST WITH strain_nack_threshold IN TANDEM A 60% threshold with a 1-minute cooldown is not very protective — the pattern can execute again quickly. A 40% threshold with a 30-minute cooldown is very conservative. Typical: 60%/5min (default) for active trading; 50%/15min for cautious sessions.
vpin_recovery_min 1 – 15 minutes default: 3 minutes Guardian

After a VPIN spike above the HighVol threshold, the Guardian continues applying full VPIN size penalty until VPIN has been continuously below the HighVol threshold for this many minutes. Without this, the system would snap back to full-size trades immediately after a VPIN spike, re-entering when conditions may still be fragile.

Treat this as a "cool-down runway" after volatility. A 3-minute recovery window means 3 minutes of clean VPIN before resuming normal sizing. For more nervous setups or after major macro events, raise to 5–8 minutes.
  • If you raise vpin_recovery_min, also consider raising vpin_highvol_threshold — otherwise minor VPIN fluctuations keep resetting the recovery clock.

NETWORK / LATENCY

latency_passive_ceiling_ms 50 – 500 ms default: 150 ms Proprioceptor

When the EWMA exchange latency exceeds this, the Proprioceptor fires FORCE_PASSIVE: the Metabolic filter blocks ALL execution (not just takers — makers too). Recovery requires latency to drop below 70% of this ceiling (latency_passive_ceiling_ms × 0.7) before execution resumes.

Entry: exchange_latency_EWMA > latency_passive_ceiling_ms Exit: exchange_latency_EWMA < latency_passive_ceiling_ms × 0.70
⚠ THE 70% HYSTERESIS IS REAL If ceiling=150ms and you are at 155ms EWMA, you are in FORCE_PASSIVE. You will not exit until latency drops below 105ms. This is intentional — rapid oscillation around the ceiling would cause rapid execution start/stop cycles. If you are seeing frequent FORCE_PASSIVE with clean market conditions, raise this ceiling.
  • If you raise latency_passive_ceiling_ms, also review halted_latency_ms — keep a minimum 3× ratio (e.g. ceiling=200ms → halted=600ms).
clock_skew_ceiling_ms 10 – 200 ms default: 50 ms Proprioceptor

Clock skew between the local clock and the exchange timestamp. High skew means order timestamps are unreliable — market-making strategies and arb signals based on timing edges become dangerous. Triggers FORCE_PASSIVE independently of latency.

Enable NTP sync on the host machine before touching this parameter. High clock skew is almost always a system configuration issue, not a trading tuning issue. If you are consistently seeing 80ms+ skew on good hardware, check NTP daemon status.
halted_latency_ms 100 – 2000 ms default: 500 ms Guardian

When exchange EWMA latency exceeds this, the Guardian transitions to HALTED state. HALTED blocks ALL intents (buys and sells) and is the only Guardian state that requires a manual RESET from the PHIC panel to clear — it does not auto-recover when latency drops. This is intentional: high-latency execution may have already sent orders the system doesn't know the outcome of.

🚨 MANUAL RESET REQUIRED AFTER HALT After latency recovers, check the exchange directly for any open/partially-filled orders before clicking RESET. The HALTED state protects you from double-execution of orders whose fill status is unknown.
  • Keep halted_latency_ms at least 2× latency_passive_ceiling_ms. Otherwise HALTED can fire before FORCE_PASSIVE, and you have no warning state.

CORRELATION

correlation_enabled true / false default: true Correlation

Enables the cross-pair signal pipeline (BTC/USDT ↔ BTC/ZAR via Binance). When enabled, the correlation worker emits regime hints and ARBI_CROSS_EXCHANGE signals that can provide aliveness boosts and execution signals. Disable if the Binance feed is unavailable or the lag measurement is unreliable.

rvr_threshold 0.5 – 3.0 default: 1.5 Correlation

Relative Volatility Ratio threshold. When the cross-pair RVR exceeds this, the cross-pair regime hint resolves to HighVol (momentum boost applies). Lower values make HighVol cross-pair hints more common; higher values reserve them for genuinely elevated relative volatility.

pearson_threshold 0.1 – 0.9 default: 0.5 Correlation

Pearson correlation coefficient threshold. When cross-pair correlation drops below this, the regime hint resolves to Crisis (correlation breakdown = elevated risk). The ARBI_CROSS_EXCHANGE pattern relies on correlation being present — low Pearson means the lag signal is noise, not edge.

cross_pair_boost 0.0 – 0.10 default: 0.04 Correlation

Aliveness boost per cross-pair signal confirmation. When a cross-pair signal agrees with a local signal on direction, the local pattern's aliveness is nudged upward by this amount. This makes cross-pair confirmed signals larger (via conviction = aliveness²).

⚠ SMALL VALUES ARE INTENTIONAL At 0.04, it takes 25 consecutive confirming cross-pair signals to move aliveness from 0 to 1.0. Raising to 0.10 means 10 signals can fully activate a pattern. Only raise if the Binance feed quality is verified (low lag measurement error).

AI JURY & GUARDIAN

inference_fidelity 0.0 – 1.0 default: 0.50 Jury

Controls how many parallel ONNX inference workers form the AI Jury. Formula: N = max(1, round(fidelity × min(hardwareConcurrency − 2, 4))). On a 4-core machine: fidelity=0.5 → 1 worker; fidelity=1.0 → 2 workers. On an 8-core machine: fidelity=0.5 → 1 worker; fidelity=1.0 → 3 workers. Multiple workers vote; the weighted consensus is used as the final p_up signal.

⚠ FIDELITY > 0.5 ON LOW-END HARDWARE Each inference worker uses ~300MB RAM and pins a CPU core. On a Mac Mini M4 (8 cores), fidelity=1.0 spawns 3 workers (cores 1–3 pinned to ONNX). Leave 2 cores free for the OS, browser, and mesh networking. Never exceed fidelity=0.75 on <4 core machines.
guardian_mode "shadow" / "active" default: "shadow" Guardian

In shadow mode, the Guardian evaluates every intent but never blocks. "Would have NACKed" decisions are logged as guardian_shadow_log events. This is a 24-hour calibration mode: run shadow first to verify Guardian isn't killing winners. In active mode, the Guardian enforces — intents it NACKs never reach the exchange.

RECOMMENDED WORKFLOW
1. Start in shadow mode for at least one trading session.
2. Review the Guardian Log — are "would have NACKed" events correct?
3. If regret is low (NACKed trades were losers), activate.
4. Monitor for missed winners (ghost trades in the Forensic Log).
⚠ LATENCY IN SHADOW MODE IS ZERO Shadow mode uses a fast-path: the intent is dispatched immediately, and a copy is sent to the Guardian in parallel for evaluation. There is no added latency in shadow mode. Active mode adds ~1ms postMessage round-trip latency to every intent — measure this against your typical VALR order submission latency before activating.
jury_entropy_threshold 0.0 – 1.0 (std dev of p_up votes) default: 0.15 Jury

When jury_size > 1, the Guardian monitors the standard deviation of individual worker p_up votes. High std dev means the jury is split — the model is uncertain. If this threshold is exceeded, the Guardian transitions to CAUTIOUS state.

⚠ ONLY RELEVANT WITH fidelity > 0.5 With fidelity=0.5 on a 4-core machine, jury_size=1 — there is no disagreement possible. This threshold only fires when at least 2 workers are active.
mesh_heat_threshold 0.0 – 1.0 (fraction of tabs) default: 0.80 Guardian

"Thundering Herd" protection. When more than this fraction of open browser tabs are simultaneously signaling BUY, the Guardian halves the size of buy intents. Prevents correlated entry across tabs (same inference model, same tick data → all tabs signal simultaneously → oversized aggregate position).

Only fires when 2+ tabs are open. At 80%, 5 out of 5 tabs must all signal BUY before the size reduction applies. With 2 tabs: any disagreement prevents triggering. Lower if you see correlated entries across tabs frequently.
house_money_threshold + house_lock_frac threshold: 0–∞ ($) / lock_frac: 0.0–1.0 default: $100 / 0.50 Guardian

House Money Protocol: once banked_profit exceeds house_money_threshold, the Guardian locks house_lock_frac of banked profits from risk. Available capital for Kelly sizing = total_value − (banked_profit × house_lock_frac), floored at 20% of total value so the system always has minimum trading capital.

After $100 banked, lock_frac=0.5: locked = banked_profit × 0.50 available = max(total_value − locked, total_value × 0.20) size_mult = min(1.0, available / total_value)
⚠ LOCK_FRAC AT 1.0 CAN STARVE EXECUTION If you lock 100% of banked profit, and banked profit is large, almost no capital is available for new trades. The 20% floor prevents total starvation but you may see very small orders. Typical: 0.50 (default) — lock half, trade the other half.
rolling_sharpe_floor -∞ – 0 (negative value) default: -0.5 Guardian

If the rolling 60-minute Sharpe ratio drops below this, the Guardian transitions to CAUTIOUS. Computed from 1-minute PnL deltas (requires ≥10 data points = 10 minutes of operation). A Sharpe of -0.5 means the strategy is consistently losing relative to its own volatility.

Sharpe ValueMeaningGuardian Response
+1.0+Strong consistent returnsNo action
0 – +1.0Positive but noisyNo action
-0.5 – 0Losing but within toleranceNo action (above floor)
Below -0.5Systematic losses→ CAUTIOUS (size reduction)
During initial warmup (first 10 minutes of session), the Sharpe is not computed. The floor does not fire on first-trade losses — only on a sustained negative trend.

CONSENSUS & VETOING

min_consensus_pct 0.0 – 100.0 default: 60.0% Mesh

Minimum fraction of mesh peers that must agree on a pattern before it can execute. With 1 peer (solo): consensus is not enforced (0 peers = no quorum). With 3+ peers: at least 60% must have the pattern in an active/healthy state. Contested echoes (below 0.70 aliveness when peers disagree) face a stricter 0.70 aliveness threshold.

vetoed_patterns list of pattern IDs default: [] Exec

Patterns in this list are immediately dropped by the Metabolic filter — they never reach execution regardless of aliveness, regime, or VPIN state. Use for permanent pattern suppression when a strategy has been found to be unreliable for the current instrument. The pattern continues to receive echo updates (it doesn't vanish from the echo table) — you can un-veto later and aliveness history is preserved.

CRITICAL COMBINATION WARNINGS

🚨 THE TRIPLE-UNLOCK MISTAKE
Raising autonomy_level, max_position_pct, AND max_total_exposure_pct simultaneously is the fastest path to a forced freeze. Each one alone is manageable; all three together create very large positions with a low floor.

Example: autonomy=1.0, max_position=50%, max_total=60% → a single winning streak builds a 60% BTC position; any reversal must erase 60% of HWM before the floor fires. At BTC prices, that can happen in minutes.
⚠ VPIN THRESHOLD vs REGIME STRAIN: CALIBRATE TOGETHER
If you run the calibration wizard after a volatile session, it may both raise vpin_crisis_threshold (e.g. to 0.87) and raise Crisis regime_strain_exp (e.g. to 2.0). These compound: the threshold becomes easy to miss and the hurdle is huge when you do miss it. After calibration, always eyeball the VPIN histogram and ask: "does this threshold still represent genuinely toxic conditions for this instrument?"
⚠ PROFIT BANKING vs STOP-LOSS ORDERING
Your profit banking must be reachable before your stop fires. A simple sanity check:
bank_profit_threshold_pct × 5 > stop_loss_pct
i.e. the stop must be at least 5 "banking thresholds" away from entry so profits are banked before a reversal hits the stop. If this is violated, you accumulate losses with no banked offsets.
🚨 LATENCY THRESHOLDS: MAINTAIN THE 0.7× EXIT RULE
FORCE_PASSIVE exits at latency_passive_ceiling_ms × 0.70. If you set latency_passive_ceiling_ms = 200ms, the system won't resume execution until latency is below 140ms. If your normal operational latency is 145ms, you will be permanently in FORCE_PASSIVE.
Rule: set ceiling 30% above your measured operational EWMA latency. Measure under real trading load, not just idle connection tests.

🚨 BEFORE CHANGING ANY PARAMETER IN A LIVE SESSION
1. Note the current portfolio value and HWM before any change.
2. Change one parameter at a time — not multiple simultaneously.
3. Wait 2–5 minutes after each change to observe the effect before the next.
4. Keep the Guardian in shadow mode until you have verified the new config is working.
5. Never raise max_total_exposure_pct during a losing session.
